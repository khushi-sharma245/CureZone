Aspirin

Aspirin is commonly used to reduce pain, fever, or inflammation. It is also an anti-inflammatory drug and can prevent blood clots.

What is aspirin used for?

Aspirin can be used for:

  • Relieving headaches
  • Reducing muscle pain
  • Managing arthritis pain
It is also used in low doses to prevent heart attacks and strokes by reducing blood clot formation.

Types of aspirin

Aspirin is available in various forms, such as:

  • Tablets
  • Chewable tablets
  • Enteric-coated tablets (to reduce stomach irritation)
  • Suppositories

How to take aspirin

Dosage depends on age, weight, and the condition being treated. Always follow the instructions on the packaging or consult a doctor.

Adults

Typical adult doses for pain or fever are 300mg to 600mg every 4 to 6 hours. For heart attack or stroke prevention, low doses (typically 75mg to 100mg daily) are used.

Possible side effects

Side effects from aspirin can include:

  • Stomach pain
  • Indigestion
  • Nausea
  • Bleeding risks
  • Allergic reactions

Warnings and precautions

Aspirin may not be suitable for people with:

  • Stomach ulcers
  • Bleeding disorders
  • Asthma
  • Liver or kidney issues
Consult your doctor before taking aspirin.
