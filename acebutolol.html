Acebutolol

Acebutolol is a beta-blocker used to treat high blood pressure and irregular heartbeats (arrhythmia). It helps to prevent strokes, heart attacks, and kidney problems by lowering blood pressure.

What is Acebutolol used for?

Acebutolol can be used to manage:

  • High blood pressure (hypertension)
  • Irregular heartbeat (arrhythmia)
  • Angina (chest pain)
This medication helps reduce the workload on the heart and makes it beat more regularly.

Types of Acebutolol

Acebutolol is typically available in:

  • Tablets
  • Extended-release capsules

How to take Acebutolol

Follow your doctor's prescription for dosing. Typically, it is taken once or twice a day with or without food. Do not skip doses and continue taking even if you feel well to maintain heart and blood pressure health.

Possible side effects

Common side effects may include:

  • Dizziness
  • Tiredness
  • Nausea
Serious side effects, though rare, may include worsening heart failure or breathing issues. Contact your doctor if you experience these.

Warnings and precautions

Before taking Acebutolol, consult your doctor if you have:

  • A history of asthma or other breathing disorders
  • Diabetes (Acebutolol may mask low blood sugar symptoms)
  • Liver or kidney disease
Do not stop taking Acebutolol suddenly, as this can worsen your condition.
