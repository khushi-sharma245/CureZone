Anastrozole

Anastrozole is a medication used primarily to treat breast cancer in women after menopause. It works by lowering estrogen levels, which can slow the growth of certain types of breast tumors.

What is Anastrozole used for?

Anastrozole is used to treat:

  • Hormone receptor-positive breast cancer
  • Advanced breast cancer in postmenopausal women
It helps reduce the risk of cancer recurrence by blocking the production of estrogen.

How to take Anastrozole

Anastrozole is usually taken as a 1mg tablet once a day, with or without food. It is important to take it regularly and at the same time every day for optimal effectiveness.

Possible side effects

Common side effects of Anastrozole include:

  • Hot flashes
  • Joint pain or stiffness
  • Weakness
  • Osteoporosis or bone thinning
Rare but serious side effects include heart disease and liver problems.

Warnings and precautions

Before taking Anastrozole, consult a doctor if you have:

  • Osteoporosis or low bone density
  • Heart disease
  • High cholesterol levels
Regular monitoring of bone density and cholesterol levels may be necessary during treatment.
