Crocin

Crocin is a brand of paracetamol, commonly used to treat mild to moderate pain, such as headaches and body aches, as well as fever.

What is Crocin used for?

Crocin can be used to relieve:

  • Headaches
  • Fever
  • Muscle pain
  • Joint pain
Crocin is one of the most commonly used painkillers and is available over the counter.

Types of Crocin

Crocin comes in various forms, including:

  • Tablets
  • Syrup (for children)
  • Rapid-release tablets

How to take Crocin

For adults, the usual dose is 500mg to 1000mg every 4 to 6 hours as needed, but no more than 4000mg in 24 hours. For children, follow the doctor's advice or the dosing guidelines on the packaging.

Possible side effects

Side effects are rare but may include:

  • Allergic reactions (skin rash, swelling)
  • Liver damage with overdose
Always follow the recommended dose to avoid adverse effects.

Warnings and precautions

Consult a doctor before taking Crocin if you have:

  • Liver or kidney problems
  • Chronic alcohol use
Avoid taking more than the recommended dose as it can lead to serious liver damage.
