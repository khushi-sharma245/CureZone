Felodipine

Felodipine is a medication used to treat high blood pressure (hypertension). It belongs to the class of drugs known as calcium channel blockers, which work by relaxing the blood vessels so the heart doesn't have to pump as hard.

What is Felodipine used for?

Felodipine can be used to treat:

  • High blood pressure (hypertension)
  • Chest pain (angina)
It helps lower the risk of strokes, heart attacks, and kidney problems.

Types of Felodipine

Felodipine is typically available in:

  • Extended-release tablets

How to take Felodipine

The usual starting dose for adults is 5 mg once daily. It can be taken with or without food, but should not be crushed or chewed. Follow your doctor's instructions closely.

Possible side effects

Side effects may include:

  • Headache
  • Dizziness
  • Flushing
Serious side effects like severe dizziness or fainting should be reported to a doctor immediately.

Warnings and precautions

Consult a doctor before taking Felodipine if you have:

  • Liver problems
  • Heart disease
Avoid consuming grapefruit or grapefruit juice while taking this medication, as it can increase the risk of side effects.
